Abstract


3D-aware GANs with generative neural radiance fields (GNeRF) have achieved impressive high-quality image generation with strong 3D consistency, especially face generation. However, most of these models focus on improving view consistency but neglect the model's disentanglement and cannot handle high-quality controllable generation. To achieve a 3D-aware generative model with controllable and disentangled ability, we introduce a conditional GNeRF model with specific attributes as input. Without re-training the entire model, we utilize the pre-trained 3D-aware model as the basis and integrate a dual-branches attribute-editing module (DAEM), taking attribute labels as inputs to achieve this goal. Moreover, we propose a Triot (Training as Init, and Optimizing for Tuning) method to train DAEM and then optimize the latent vector to preserve the face identity further. Extensive experiments on the widely used FFHQ show that our model yields better 3D consistent face editing results while preserving well the non-target regions comparing to existing methods.

Double-branch Attribute Editing

We propose a dual branches attribute-editing module (DAEM) to promote the controllability and disentanglement of the 3D-aware generative model.

Triot: Training-as-Init, Optimizing-for-Tuning

We present a novel learning method, `Training-as-Init, Optimizing-for-Tuning' (Triot), combining the model-training and latent-optimizing method for the attribute-editing task.

In detail, we take the edited latent vector for target attributes as init, fix the entire model, then optimize the latent vector with the proposed consistency losses to search for better latent code.
